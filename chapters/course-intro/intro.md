# Course Introduction

No matter what field you enter, you will interact with data every day. Data management sits at the heart of every successful business and is a fundamental component of business analytics — a working knowledge of database management systems, data warehousing, Hadoop, graph databases, and vector stores raises your professional value whether you code daily or operate at an executive level.

## Course topic mix

This course allocates time across seven topic areas, weighted toward the essentials that analysts use every week:

|---|---|---|

SQL is the single most-used skill in the course because it is the single most-used skill in industry. Later topics (graph, big data, vector) are lighter but increasingly important — they're where the field is moving.

## Why this matters for your career

Look at the top in-demand tech roles — cybersecurity, software development, cloud, ML, data science, AI — and every one of them assumes fluency in at least one of the data layers taught here. Even in executive roles, knowing *how* your team creates deliverables and *why* they choose one data architecture over another is often necessary to steer the work.

## On using Generative AI

GenAI tools (Gemini, ChatGPT, Grok, Claude) are useful — and you *must* learn to use them well. The course's position in three lines:

1. **GenAI is an assistant, not an oracle.** You have the ultimate authority and responsibility for your work.
2. **Don't copy-paste.** Read what the tool suggests, understand it, adapt it. If you don't understand the suggestion, don't use it.
3. **Augment, don't replace.** Learn the concepts first so you can solve problems *without* GenAI. Then use GenAI to validate, clarify, and move faster.

Models are trained on human-generated data and inherit human biases and inaccuracies. Treat their output as a first draft that you own and edit.
